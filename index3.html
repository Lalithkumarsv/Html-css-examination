A burger restaurant is a place where you can eat a meal and pay for it, and some examples include gourmet, upscale, chicken, greasy, and homemade burger restaurants.

The modern hamburger originated in the United States. Louis Lassen, a Danish immigrant, is credited with creating the hamburger steak sandwich in 1900.

Burgers can be made from ground or minced beef, but can also be made with other ingredients like turkey, tofu, or lamb

Some burger trends include using exotic toppings like goat cheese and truffle oil, focusing on international flavors, and adding eggs and protein boosters

Burgers are higher in calories and protein than pizza, but there are substitutes that can make them healthier.
